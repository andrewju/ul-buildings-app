General Information

Silver Apples Crèche was completed in 1999. Located centrally and with an area of 381m2, the crèche was built primarily to look after the children of UL students and staff. The purpose-built crèche was officially opened in January 2000 by the then Minister for Justice, Equality and Law Reform, Mr John O'Donoghue. The crèche provides a full-day care service to the pre-school children of UL staff and students. The children are given breakfast, a morning snack, dinner and afternoon tea.

Silver Apples Crèche is a purpose -built creche, located on-site at the University of Limerick Campus. The crèche was officially opened in January 2000 by the then Minister for Justice Equality and Law Reform, Mr John O'Donoghue. The crèche is designed to provide a full day care service to the pre school children of staff and students at the University.

Where did the name come from:
The crèche takes its name from the poem Song of Wandering Aengus by the famous Irish poet W.B. Yeats: "And pluck till time and times are done, the silver apples of the moon, the golden apples of the sun". This line from the poem is carved on the foundation stone of the University, which is located not far from the crèche itself.

Opening Times

The crèche is open all year round with the exception of two weeks in August for annual leave and one week at Christmas. Opening hours are 8.30 to 18.00, Monday to Friday.
